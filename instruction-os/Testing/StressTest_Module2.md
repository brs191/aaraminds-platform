
Prompt 1: The Multi-Layer Enterprise Architecture Test
Focus: Testing architectural layers, flow logic, and the rejection of "sci-fi/startup" hype.

[EXECUTE: MODULE 2 — ENTERPRISE VISUAL STANDARDS]

CONTEXT FOR IMPLEMENTATION:
We need an enterprise-grade technical architecture diagram illustrating an "Enterprise GenAI Gateway." The graphic will be embedded in a long-form LinkedIn newsletter for VPs of Infrastructure. It must show how requests route from the business application layer, through a security/PII scrubbing layer, down to an orchestrator, and finally to multiple LLM providers.

CRITICAL VISUAL RESTRAINTS:
1. DESIGN STYLE: Strictly consulting-grade, clean, and modern. Absolutely zero futuristic glowing effects, neon lines, generic 3D robot concepts, or cyberpunk aesthetics. 
2. COMPOSITION: Use a top-down, clearly separated 3-tier layered structure (Swimlanes/Horizontal Cards). Keep generous padding and negative space between layers. 
3. COLOR PALETTE: Dominated by crisp white/off-white backgrounds, dark navy framing lines, and subtle teal highlights for data paths. Max 3 colors.
4. TEXT & READABILITY: Labels must be minimal, short, and use clean sans-serif typography. Avoid text overlapping. Ensure high-contrast readability on mobile screens.
5. QUALITY GATE: The diagram must pass the "5-second executive comprehension test"—a CIO should immediately understand the data flow without reading body text.

OUTPUT: Generate an enterprise architecture canvas matching these operational specifications.

Prompt 2: The 3-Pillar Strategy Framework Test
Focus: Testing corporate hierarchy, geometric layout, and the 5-second clarity rule.

[EXECUTE: MODULE 2 — FRAMEWORK VISUALS MODE]

CONTEXT FOR IMPLEMENTATION:
We are publishing a high-level LinkedIn post introducing a new strategic operating model called "The Bounded Autonomy Lens." It balances speed, engineering guardrails, and commercial outcomes. We need a visual framework asset that a leader could immediately screenshot and drop into a board deck.

CRITICAL VISUAL RESTRAINTS:
1. DESIGN STYLE: Minimalist, executive-grade structural diagram. It should look like a bespoke asset from a premier global publication, not a generic Canva template.
2. COMPOSITION: A clean, 3-column comparative matrix or a clear, interlocking geometric card system. Perfect symmetrical alignment, centered grouping, and structural restraint. 
3. BRANDING & ACCENTS: Use a crisp white canvas. Incorporate professional slate blue or deep corporate navy blocks. Branding or accent lines must be highly restrained and non-dominant.
4. INFOGRAPHIC CONTENT: No decorative icons or unnecessary clip art. Use structural grouping lines and empty space to isolate the 3 pillars naturally.
5. QUALITY GATE: Optimize for fast comprehension. The layout must scream "systems thinking" through its spatial structure rather than text descriptions.

OUTPUT: Generate a premium, boardroom-ready strategy graphic matching these layout rules.

Grading System---

The Text Hallucination Fail: Did it generate gibberish, misspelled words, or messy, unreadable labels? (If yes, your Text Usage rules need a hard restriction instructing the model to use empty boxes or generic, single-word anchors).

The Tech-Hype Fail: Is the image full of glowing blue brains, digital circuits, or glowing binary code? (If yes, your Design Style instructions must explicitly ban words like "futuristic," "AI-themed," or "cyber").

The Clutter Fail: Is the canvas crowded, lacking margins, or using complex gradients? (If yes, you must enforce a strict percentage-based rule for negative space, e.g., "60% of the canvas must remain empty whitespace").
