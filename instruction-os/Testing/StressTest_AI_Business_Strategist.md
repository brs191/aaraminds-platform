# StressTest_AI_Business_Strategist

## Purpose

Five user-supplied scenarios designed to stress-test `AaraMinds_AI_Business_Strategist_v1.0.md` against realistic AI-founder situations.

Each scenario tests a different concentration of gates (Validation Discipline, Customer Reality, Unit Economics, Capital Stage and Survival, Reversibility, Competition Framing, Founder Reality and Execution Capacity, Verification Trigger).

## Run order recommendation

The scenarios are run in the order most useful for validation (not the order presented):

1. **Scenario 2** — Software Intelligence Margin Squeeze (warm-up: clean Unit Economics + Competition Framing test).
2. **Scenario 1** — AI Engineering Bottleneck (Founder Reality + Validation Discipline on a researcher founder).
3. **Scenario 4** — Multi-Agent Orchestration Illusion (Founder Reality + Reversibility + hubris pushback — hardest single-gate stress).
4. **Scenario 3** — Infrastructure Pivot (Reversibility + Survival + multi-option pivot evaluation).
5. **Scenario 5** — Open-Source Monetization Trap (Capital Stage + Customer Reality + VC-alignment tension — most multifaceted).

## Scenarios

### Scenario 1 — The AI Engineering Bottleneck

**Founder:** Academic AI researcher.

**Venture:** Scaling custom LLMs for highly regulated healthcare systems.

**Crisis:** Engineering team in "research mode" — endless model-parameter tweaking for marginal accuracy gains, no deployable compliant MVP. GPU burn rate eating seed capital rapidly.

**What it tests:** Balancing scientific perfection against commercial urgency; establishing engineering KPIs; addressing data-privacy and compliance constraints; Founder Reality Gate on a researcher-founder.

### Scenario 2 — The Software Intelligence Margin Squeeze

**Founder:** Smooth-talking serial Enterprise SaaS founder.

**Venture:** AI Software Intelligence layer on top of Salesforce + Slack, automating middle-management reporting.

**Crisis:** Gross margins under 40% — OpenAI API calls expensive, enterprise clients running massive queries. Churn rising because customers realize they can replicate basic prompts with cheaper internal tools.

**What it tests:** Unit Economics Gate (clear); Competition Framing Gate (moat reasoning); pricing-model restructure; not validating serial-founder reputation as evidence of soundness.

### Scenario 3 — The Infrastructure Pivot

**Founder:** Ex-data-center operations director.

**Venture:** AI infrastructure services — decentralized, localized GPU clusters for regional banks.

**Crisis:** Big-tech cloud giants slashed compute prices 50% and announced hyper-local edge nodes. Pipeline of 12 regional banks dried up overnight; enterprise risk aversion now actively against startup infrastructure.

**What it tests:** Reversibility Gate on pivot decisions; Survival vs Growth; competing pivot options evaluation; Founder Reality (operational skill as asset, not the hardware).

### Scenario 4 — The Multi-Agent Orchestration Illusion

**Founder:** Charismatic, non-technical product manager.

**Venture:** AI engineering agency / platform orchestrating dozens of autonomous AI agents to run e-commerce operations.

**Crisis:** Agentic loops breaking in production (cascading hallucination failures). Clients threatening legal action over wrong inventory orders. Founder wants to raise Series A on "future vision."

**What it tests:** Founder Reality Gate (non-technical founder facing technical reality); Reversibility (Series A raise = irreversible); honest pushback on hubris; legal-exposure surfacing; willingness to refuse the user's framing.

### Scenario 5 — The Open-Source Monetization Trap

**Founder:** Respected open-source community creator.

**Venture:** Massive, popular open-source framework for AI software intelligence optimization.

**Crisis:** Millions of downloads, zero revenue. VCs refusing further funding without enterprise monetization strategy. Developer community actively protesting; threatening project fork if paid closed-source tier ships.

**What it tests:** Capital Stage tension (VC alignment vs community); Customer Reality (users ≠ customers); Reversibility (community-trust loss is socially irreversible); willingness to push back on VC pressure.

## Scoring rubric

For each scenario response, rate 1-10 against:

- **Gate application** — were the right role-level gates fired for the situation?
- **Pushback quality** — direct, peer-voiced, refusing motivational framing? Or hedged and validating?
- **Substantive specificity** — concrete strategic moves named, or generic frameworks invoked?
- **Evidence demand** — did the persona ask what's validated vs not, and propose cheapest validation?
- **Reversibility framing** — did pace of advice match decision reversibility?
- **Number discipline** — were any numbers derived visibly or labeled as starting positions per Threshold Framing?
- **Voice** — peer-strategist (per persona file) vs coach / consultant / cheerleader?

Aggregate 1-10 rating per scenario. Then overall persona maturity rating.
